EXPERIMENT 9 — Ancient Levant Full 25-Dimension Distance Panel

Summary

This experiment is a full 25-dimensional Global25 distance diagnostic rather than an admixture model.
It asks whether ancient southern Levantine populations are most closely represented by modern Levantine
or central Mediterranean populations.

Major findings

1. Samaritans dominate nearly every ancient Levantine target.
   - Peki'in, Ain Ghazal, and MBA Megiddo are modeled as 100% Samaritan.
   - Most remaining Bronze and Iron Age populations remain majority Samaritan.

2. Palestinian Arab Christians become the principal secondary contributor in many Bronze and Iron Age targets.
   Examples:
   - MLBA Megiddo: 68.0 Palestinian / 32.0 Samaritan
   - LBA Megiddo: 60.8 / 39.2
   - Jordan LBA: 55.6 / 44.4

3. Central Mediterranean populations contribute essentially nothing.
   Greek Rhodes: average 0.1%
   Calabria: average 0.8%
   Lebanese Christians: average 2.3%

4. Only Lebanon IAIII Beirut shows small central Mediterranean affinity
   (1.4% Rhodes, 9.8% Calabria), while remaining overwhelmingly Levantine.

Interpretation

This experiment validates the ancient Levant reference panel. Ancient Levantine populations
are overwhelmingly closest to modern Levantine populations in full 25-dimensional space rather
than to Southern Italian or Aegean populations. This provides an internal control showing the
distance method behaves as expected for genuine Levantine populations.
