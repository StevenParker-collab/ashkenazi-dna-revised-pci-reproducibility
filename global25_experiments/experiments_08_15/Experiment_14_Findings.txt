EXPERIMENT 14 — REGIONAL ASHKENAZI FULL MODERN PANEL WITH LEVANT CONTROLS

Purpose

The identical full modern source panel from Experiment 10 is applied to ten
named regional Ashkenazi population averages. No individual is used.

The regional series tests whether the pooled Experiment 10 structure remains
stable across western and eastern Ashkenazi populations, and whether later
Northern or Eastern European displacement replaces the older Italki and
central Mediterranean signal.

Major finding 7: regional variation redistributes correlated Mediterranean
proxies without eliminating the underlying block

The detailed coefficients vary:

France and Germany use large Maltese, Calabrian, and Campanian combinations.

Lithuania and Russia use West Sicily.

Romania alone retains Rhodes.

Austria uses Maltese and Lombardy.

Belarus uses Calabria and Campania.

Poland and Latvia place especially large coefficients directly on Italki.

This is expected in a correlated modern competition panel. Italki, Southern
Italian, Sicilian, Maltese, and Aegean sources overlap and can substitute for
one another. The robust signal is the regional block total, not the exact
allocation to one modern province or island.

Major finding 8: Italki is the most persistent single source in the panel

Italki is retained in 10 of 10 regional targets.

Maltese and Druze are each retained in 9 of 10. German is also retained in
9 of 10, but at a much lower average of 7.9 compared with 38.1 for Italki.

The combination of universal persistence, the highest mean coefficient, and
largest-source status in nine regions makes Italki the clearest individual
anchor of the regional series.

Major finding 10: the regional series reinforces Experiments 10 through 13

Experiment 10:
The pooled Ashkenazi target selects Italki as the dominant source.

Experiment 11:
Removing Italki worsens the fit and transfers most of its coefficient to
Maltese, Sicilian, and Italian sources rather than to Northern/Eastern Europe.

Experiment 12:
Removing the broader central Mediterranean source space sharply worsens the
fit and inflates Lombardy and Levantine proxies.

Experiment 13:
Italki itself independently resolves primarily through Maltese, Calabrian,
Sicilian, and Italian space with no retained Northern/Eastern European source.

Experiment 14:
The same general structure is reproduced across ten regional Ashkenazi
population averages.

Together, the experiments show that the pooled result is not being driven by
one regional subgroup or by aggregation. The Italki and central Mediterranean
signal is geographically persistent across the Ashkenazi regional series.

Conclusion

Experiment 14 provides strong regional validation of the pooled Ashkenazi
result.

Across all ten regional targets:

1. Italki is retained every time.
2. Italki is the largest individual source in nine of ten regions.
3. The strict central Mediterranean block is a majority in every region.
4. The strict block averages 58.6.
5. The broader Italian and central Mediterranean block averages 62.5.
6. Levantine continuity averages 23.5.
7. Northern/Eastern Europe averages 14.0 and remains a minority in every
   region.

The regional series is consistent with a shared Italian Jewish and central
Mediterranean foundation that predates later Ashkenazi dispersal. Regional
European admixture modifies the profile but does not create or replace its
dominant structure.

Methodological caution

The displayed values are constrained similarity coefficients, not exact
historical ancestry percentages. Closely related modern sources can substitute
for one another, and the regional averages have unequal sample sizes.

The strongest evidence is therefore not one coefficient in isolation. It is
the repeated category-level result across ten independently modeled regional
population averages using the same source panel.
