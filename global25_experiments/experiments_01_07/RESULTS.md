# Recorded Results

These are descriptive outputs from source-panel-dependent Global25 models. They are not literal descent percentages and do not supply confidence intervals or formal significance tests.

## Experiment 1 — Expanded controlled Natufian panel

The raw export contains 196 substantive targets plus the calculator's final Average row. Every target used the same seven explicitly named prehistoric sources.

### Restricted screenshot versus controlled replication

| Comparison | n | Mean Natufian | Range |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Reddit screenshot: Calabria plus eastern and western Sicily | 14 | 7.04% | 4.6%–9.8% |
| Controlled run: the identical fourteen target labels | 14 | 8.39% | 6.2%–12.2% |

Twelve of the fourteen controlled values are higher than their screenshot counterparts, two are unchanged, and none is lower. The target names are identical, so the shift cannot be explained by swapping the Southern Italian target rows. The documented differences are the source construction and panel design: the screenshot uses hidden broad aliases and gives WHG only to the Southern Italian run, while the replication gives the same seven explicit sources to every target.

The screenshot did include every individual visible under its chosen eight Calabrian, three eastern Sicilian, and three western Sicilian labels. The defensible criticism is therefore comparison-frame selection, not an unsupported claim that individual rows were secretly deleted within those labels.

### Complete range table

| Complete target set | n | Mean Natufian | Range | Width |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Campania | 26 | 7.13% | 2.4%–11.0% | 8.6 points |
| Calabria | 8 | 7.65% | 6.2%–8.8% | 2.6 points |
| Eastern Sicily | 3 | 9.13% | 6.6%–10.6% | 4.0 points |
| Western Sicily | 3 | 9.60% | 6.4%–12.2% | 5.8 points |
| Southern Italy combined | 40 | 7.57% | 2.4%–12.2% | 9.8 points |
| Malta | 8 | 9.30% | 4.0%–16.4% | 12.4 points |
| Crete | 111 | 7.24% | 0.0%–13.4% | 13.4 points |
| Dodecanese, Rhodes, and Kos | 26 | 7.72% | 0.4%–12.0% | 11.6 points |

Italki Jews receive 16.2% Natufian. The gap from the Southern Italian high of 12.2% is 4.0 points, while the internal Southern Italian range is 9.8 points. The range-to-gap ratio is therefore 2.45. A rule that treats the smaller Jewish-to-regional gap as proof against common origin would first split the Southern Italian comparison set itself.

The ten regional Ashkenazi population averages have a mean of 13.24% and span 11.4%–15.0%. The separate matched pooled Ashkenazi run is 12.8% Natufian and 14.8% EHG at distance 0.02922803. It is only 0.6 points above the Southern Italian individual high and falls inside the Maltese and Cretan ranges. Italki at 16.2% falls inside the observed Maltese range, whose maximum is 16.4%.

Adding Campania lowers the selected Calabria/Sicily mean while widening the range. The methodological point is not that every omitted population must raise a mean; it is that a restricted mean is not a biological maximum and one component cannot decide historical common origin.

## Experiment 2 — Ashkenazi full modern competition

The pooled Ashkenazi target has distance 0.00948576. The selected model is 58.2% Italki Jewish, 18.6% Rhodian Greek, 9.4% Belarusian, 5.8% Maltese, 4.6% Swedish, 3.0% central Sicilian and 0.4% Cretan Greek from Lasithi. The selected model is 29.0% Trapani Sicilian, 27.4% Maltese, 24.4% Cretan Greek from Lasithi, 12.8% central Sicilian, 6.0% Cretan Greek and 0.4% Friulian. ## Experiment 4 — Northern Italian proxy diagnostic

This is a direct-distance ranking, not a mixture table. Maltese is closest to pooled Ashkenazi Jews at 0.01918750, followed by Lasithi Cretans, Mani Greeks, general Cretans, and Italki Jews. ## Experiment 6 — Reciprocal Italki model

The Italki target has distance 0.01732664 and is modeled as 52.6% Maltese, 44.6% Rhodian Greek, 2.4% eastern Sicilian and 0.4% Reggio Calabria. No pooled Ashkenazi, Northern Italian, Tuscan, or Northern/Eastern European source receives a displayed nonzero coefficient.

## Experiment 7 — Regional Ashkenazi panel

Italki Jewish is the largest single selected source in all ten regional models, ranging from 41.2% in Germany to 70.2% in Austria. The displayed Average row assigns 57.0% to Italki Jewish. Grouped descriptively, the average is 89.5% Italian Jewish/Sicilian/Maltese/Aegean and 10.6% Northern/Eastern European; the 0.1-point excess over 100 is display rounding. No Northern Italian or Tuscan source receives a displayed nonzero coefficient in any row.
