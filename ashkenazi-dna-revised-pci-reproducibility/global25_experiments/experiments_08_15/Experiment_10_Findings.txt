EXPERIMENT 10 — ASHKENAZI FULL MODERN COMPETITION WITH LEVANT CONTROLS

Purpose

The pooled Ashkenazi Jewish average is modeled with Italki Jews, Southern Italians,
Sicilians, Maltese, Aegean Greeks, modern Levantine-continuity populations,
Northern/Central Italians, and Northern/Eastern Europeans competing simultaneously.
This is the principal modern-population stress test of the preprint thesis.

Result

Distance: 0.00819655

Retained sources:
Italki_Jew_(n=9): 40.8
Druze_Israel_(n=42): 22.8
Maltese_(n=8): 11.4
Russian_Smolensk_(n=15): 11.0
Italian_Lombardy_(Lombard)_(n=10): 9.8
Italian_Sicily_West_(Sicilian)_(n=8): 2.4
German_(n=113): 1.8

Major findings

1. Italki Jews are the dominant source.

Italki_Jew_(n=9) contributes 40.8 percent, by far the largest single coefficient.
This is especially important because Italki Jews are not a Rhineland or Eastern European
population. Their dominant role identifies an Italian Jewish and central Mediterranean
core that cannot be explained as a later Ashkenazi acquisition in Germany, Poland, or Russia.

2. The central Mediterranean block is the majority of the model.

Italki Jews, Maltese, and western Sicilians together account for 54.6 percent:
40.8 + 11.4 + 2.4 = 54.6.

Including Italian Lombardy raises the total represented by Italian, Italian Jewish,
Sicilian, and Maltese sources to 64.4 percent. Lombardy should not be treated as equivalent
to Southern Italy, but its retained coefficient still belongs to the Italian side of the
competition rather than the Levantine or Northern/Eastern European side.

3. The Levantine-continuity contribution is substantial but secondary.

Druze_Israel_(n=42) contributes 22.8 percent. Samaritan, Palestinian Arab Christian,
and Lebanese Arab Christian do not survive the simultaneous competition in the displayed
nonzero solution. Thus, the model does not describe Ashkenazi Jews as primarily modern
Levantine-like. The retained Levantine signal is real, but it is much smaller than the
combined Italian Jewish and central Mediterranean signal.

4. Northern and Eastern European sources are minority contributors.

Russian_Smolensk_(n=15) contributes 11.0 percent and German_(n=113) contributes 1.8 percent,
for a combined Northern/Eastern European contribution of 12.8 percent. Polish is not retained.
This result is incompatible with a model in which the principal Ashkenazi structure was created
in the Rhineland or Eastern Europe. Those populations contribute a minority layer after the
Italki and central Mediterranean sources have already absorbed most of the profile.

5. The Italki coefficient is larger than the combined German and Russian coefficients.

Italki: 40.8
German + Russian: 12.8

The Italki coefficient is more than three times the combined German and Russian total.
This is one of the clearest findings of the experiment.

6. The fit is strong.

The reported distance is 0.00819655. Within this calculator framework, that is a close fit
for a seven-source modern competition model. The low distance indicates that the retained
combination reproduces the pooled Ashkenazi average well.

7. Source competition matters.

Several geographically related sources receive zero because closely correlated populations
compete for the same dimensions. A zero does not prove the historical absence of ancestry from
that region. It means that another source represented the same signal more efficiently in this
particular constrained panel. The decisive feature is the structure of the retained solution:
Italki is dominant, central Mediterranean sources form the majority, modern Levantine continuity
is secondary, and Northern/Eastern Europe is a minority component.

Interpretation

Experiment 10 strongly supports an Italian Jewish and central Mediterranean foundation for
the pooled Ashkenazi population. The largest coefficient is Italki Jewish, and the majority
of the fitted profile is assigned to Italki, Maltese, Sicilian, and other Italian sources.
The model also detects a meaningful Druze-like Levantine contribution and a smaller
Northern/Eastern European layer.

The result is consistent with formation in the Greco-Roman and Italian Jewish world followed
by later admixture. It does not support a primarily Levantine population receiving only a
generic European addition, nor does it support a population principally formed in the
Rhineland or Eastern Europe.

Methodological caution

The calculator coefficients are model-dependent similarity weights, not direct historical
ancestry percentages. Modern sources are proxies, and correlated sources can substitute for
one another. Historical conclusions should therefore rest on convergence across Experiments
1–10, not on one coefficient in isolation.
