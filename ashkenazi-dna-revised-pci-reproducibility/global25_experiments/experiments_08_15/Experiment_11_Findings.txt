EXPERIMENT 11 — ASHKENAZI SAME PANEL WITH ITALKI OMITTED

Purpose

This matched omission test removes Italki_Jew_(n=9) while leaving the
Southern Italian, Sicilian, Maltese, Aegean, modern Levantine-continuity,
Northern/Central Italian, and Northern/Eastern European source classes unchanged.

Corrected displayed result

Distance: 0.00922012

Retained sources

Maltese_(n=8): 34.0
Druze_Israel_(n=42): 23.0
Italian_Lombardy_(Lombard)_(n=10): 14.4
Italian_Sicily_West_(Sicilian)_(n=8): 9.6
Russian_Smolensk_(n=15): 9.4
Palestinian_Arab_Christian_(n=24): 8.0
Samaritan_(n=7): 1.6

Major finding 1: removing Italki worsens the fit

Experiment 10 distance with Italki_Jew_(n=9): 0.00819655
Experiment 11 distance without Italki_Jew_(n=9): 0.00922012

Absolute deterioration: 0.00102357
Relative deterioration: 12.49 percent

The fit becomes clearly worse when Italki is removed. The remaining sources
cannot reproduce the pooled Ashkenazi profile as efficiently.

Major finding 2: most of the omitted Italki coefficient moves to Italian and
central Mediterranean sources

Maltese_(n=8): 11.4 to 34.0, increase of 22.6
Italian_Sicily_West_(Sicilian)_(n=8): 2.4 to 9.6, increase of 7.2
Italian_Lombardy_(Lombard)_(n=10): 9.8 to 14.4, increase of 4.6

These increases total 34.4 points.

The omitted Italki coefficient in Experiment 10 was 40.8. Therefore,
34.4 of 40.8 points, or 84.3 percent of the omitted coefficient, reappears
as increases in Maltese, Sicilian, and Italian Lombard sources.

This is a replacement-pattern statistic rather than a literal historical
ancestry percentage. Structurally, the model searches first for substitutes
in the Italian and central Mediterranean portion of the panel.

Major finding 3: Maltese is the largest replacement

Maltese rises by 22.6 points and becomes the largest retained source at 34.0.
Western Sicilian rises to 9.6.

The Maltese-plus-Sicilian block totals 43.6. Including Lombardy raises the
Italian, Sicilian, and Maltese total to 58.0.

Lombardy is not Southern Italian and should not be described as such. Its
importance here is that the omitted Italki signal is reconstructed mainly
inside the broader Italian and central Mediterranean side of the competition.

Major finding 4: the Levantine-continuity block rises but remains secondary

Experiment 10 retained 22.8 Druze.

Experiment 11 retains:

Druze_Israel_(n=42): 23.0
Palestinian_Arab_Christian_(n=24): 8.0
Samaritan_(n=7): 1.6

The corrected Levantine-continuity total is 32.6, an increase of 9.8 points
over Experiment 10.

This indicates that Italki shares some dimensions also represented by
Levantine-continuity populations. Even so, the Levantine block remains
smaller than the 58.0 assigned to Maltese, Sicilian, and Italian sources.

Major finding 5: Northern and Eastern Europe do not replace Italki

Experiment 10:

Russian_Smolensk_(n=15): 11.0
German_(n=113): 1.8
Combined: 12.8

Experiment 11:

Russian_Smolensk_(n=15): 9.4
German_(n=113): 0.0
Combined: 9.4

The Northern/Eastern European total falls by 3.4 points after Italki is
removed. Polish remains at zero.

If Italki were merely acting as a Rhineland or Eastern European proxy,
German, Polish, or Russian should rise after its removal. Instead, their
combined contribution declines.

Major finding 6: the redistribution exactly accounts for the omitted 40.8

Italian and central Mediterranean increases: +34.4
Levantine-continuity increase: +9.8
Northern/Eastern European decrease: -3.4

Net redistribution: 34.4 + 9.8 - 3.4 = 40.8

This exactly matches the Experiment 10 Italki coefficient.

The matched omission test therefore shows where the model looks when the
Italian Jewish source is unavailable:

First, overwhelmingly toward Maltese, Sicilian, and Italian sources.
Second, toward additional Levantine-continuity sources.
Not toward Germany, Poland, or Russia.

Conclusion

Experiment 11 strengthens the interpretation of Experiment 10.

Removing Italki:

1. worsens the fit by 12.49 percent;
2. transfers 34.4 points to Maltese, Sicilian, and Italian sources;
3. raises the Levantine-continuity block by 9.8 points;
4. lowers the Northern/Eastern European block by 3.4 points.

The result supports an Italian Jewish and central Mediterranean core with a
meaningful but secondary Levantine-continuity layer and a smaller later
Northern/Eastern European contribution.

Methodological caution

The displayed values are constrained model coefficients, not exact historical
ancestry percentages. The strongest inference comes from the matched change
between Experiments 10 and 11: the panel is held constant, Italki is removed,
the fit worsens, and the replacement pattern can be measured directly.
