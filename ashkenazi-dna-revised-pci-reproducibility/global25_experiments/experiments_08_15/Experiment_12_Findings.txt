EXPERIMENT 12 — ASHKENAZI SOUTHERN ITALIAN AND CENTRAL MEDITERRANEAN SOURCES OMITTED

Purpose

This negative-control panel removes Italki, Southern Italian, Sicilian,
Maltese, and Aegean averages while retaining modern Levantine-continuity,
Northern/Central Italian, and Northern/Eastern European sources.

The experiment measures two effects caused by excluding the regional source
space central to the preprint thesis:

1. the deterioration in model fit; and
2. the proxy redistribution into the remaining source classes.

Major finding 2: Lombardy becomes an inflated Italian proxy

Italian_Lombardy_(Lombard)_(n=10) rises:

Experiment 10: 9.8
Experiment 11: 14.4
Experiment 12: 38.0

Relative to Experiment 10, Lombardy gains 28.2 points.
Relative to Experiment 11, Lombardy gains 23.6 points.

This should not be interpreted as evidence that Ashkenazi Jews are 38 percent
Lombard. It is a proxy-inflation result caused by omission. Once Italki,
Southern Italian, Sicilian, Maltese, and Aegean sources are unavailable, the
optimizer forces a large part of the missing Italian and Mediterranean signal
into the nearest surviving Italian source.

This directly demonstrates how Northern Italian proxies can become artificially
prominent when Southern Italian and related central Mediterranean comparators
are excluded.

This does not mean the target suddenly becomes more Levantine when sources are

The result is therefore a direct demonstration of source-omission bias:
excluding the central Mediterranean source space creates an artificially
enlarged Levantine coefficient.

Major finding 5: the Experiment 10 central Mediterranean block is redistributed
exactly into Lombardy and Levantine proxy inflation

Italki_Jew_(n=9): 40.8
Maltese_(n=8): 11.4

Lombardy increases by 28.2

28.2 + 29.2 - 2.8 = 54.6

That exactly equals the omitted Experiment 10 Italki–Maltese–Sicilian block.

This is one of the strongest results in the matched series. Once the relevant
central Mediterranean sources are excluded, their entire 54.6-point signal is
redistributed into an inflated Northern Italian proxy and inflated Levantine
controls, while Northern/Eastern Europe declines.

Major finding 7: the omission experiment reproduces the precise bias criticized
in the preprint

The full panel in Experiment 10 identifies Italki as the dominant individual
source and assigns a majority to Italki, Maltese, and Sicilian populations.

Experiment 11 removes Italki. The model then shifts primarily toward Maltese,
Sicilian, and Italian sources, while the fit worsens.

Experiment 12 removes the broader Southern Italian and central Mediterranean
source space. The fit worsens much more sharply, Lombardy rises to 38.0, and
the Levantine-continuity block rises to 52.0.

This is exactly the structural distortion expected when the most relevant
comparators are absent: a genuinely central Mediterranean profile is forced
into an artificial Northern Italian–Levantine mixture.

Subtler findings

Italian Tuscany receives zero while Lombardy receives 38.0. This indicates that
Lombardy, not Tuscany, is the surviving Italian proxy that best compensates for
the omitted source space in this particular constrained panel. It does not make
Lombardy the true historical source.

Lebanese Arab Christian receives zero while Druze, Palestinian Arab Christian,
and Samaritan collectively absorb the Levantine-continuity side. This reflects
competition among correlated modern Levantine controls.

German and Polish receive zero, and Russian remains close to 10. This confirms
that Northern/Eastern European ancestry is a comparatively stable minority
layer rather than the source class replacing the omitted Mediterranean core.

Conclusion

Experiment 12 is a powerful negative-control and source-omission test.

Removing Italki, Southern Italian, Sicilian, Maltese, and Aegean sources:

1. worsens the fit by 47.18 percent relative to the full panel;
2. inflates Lombardy from 9.8 to 38.0;
3. inflates the modern Levantine-continuity block from 22.8 to 52.0;
4. reduces Northern/Eastern Europe from 12.8 to 10.0; and
5. exactly redistributes the missing 54.6-point Italki–Maltese–Sicilian block.

The experiment does not support a predominantly Levantine or Northern Italian
historical origin. It demonstrates that those categories become inflated
proxies when the Italian Jewish, Southern Italian, Sicilian, Maltese, and
Aegean source space is removed.

Methodological caution

The displayed coefficients are constrained similarity weights, not exact
historical ancestry percentages. The strongest inference is comparative:
the same Ashkenazi target is tested across matched panels, the relevant source
space is progressively removed, the fit deteriorates, and the replacement
pattern can be measured directly.
